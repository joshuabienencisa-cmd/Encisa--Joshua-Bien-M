Frequently Asked Questions about Generative AI

What is Generative AI?

Generative artificial intelligence (generative AI) is a type of AI that can create new content and ideas, including conversations, stories, images, videos, and music. It can learn human language, programming languages, art, chemistry, biology, or any complex subject matter. It reuses what it knows to solve new problems.

How does it work?

It’s basically a machine that is capable of doing most of the things the human can also do. Generative AI is beneficial since they can accomplish tasks much faster than humans. If they are given a certain prompt, it can be a democrat for those who don’t speak English as their first language.

What are examples of GenAI tools?

Examples of GenAI can be categorized by text-generation, image-generation, video-generation, music-generation, code generation, and productivity tools. Examples for text-generation are ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude. Examples for image-generation are Adobe Firefly and DALL-E. Examples for video-generation are Pika Labs and Lumen5. Examples for music generation are Voicemod AI and Suno AI. An example for code generation is Github Copilot. Examples of productivity tools are Notion AI and GrammarlyGO.

What can GenAI do (strengths)?

GenAI can generate essays, answer questions in seconds. It can make images, videos, and music and by that it can help people with creative work. It can also explain any lessons, fix grammar, and assist with coding. Basically GenAI is very useful for saving time.

What are its limitations or risks?

Generative AI can sometimes produce incorrect or nonsensical answers, known as "hallucinations." It may also generate biased or harmful content if trained on biased data. Additionally, there are concerns about privacy, security, and the ethical use of AI-generated content.
